# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- **Being Respectful**: Using welcoming and inclusive language
- **Being Collaborative**: Working constructively with other community members
- **Being Supportive**: Helping others learn and grow
- **Accepting Feedback**: Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- **Taking Responsibility**: Acknowledging mistakes and learning from them
- **Focusing on Community**: Prioritizing what's best for the overall community

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Sexual attention or advances of any kind
- Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Social media accounts related to the project
- Online and offline events representing the project
- Any space where you're representing the SquadUp project

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

To report a violation:

1. **GitHub**: Open an issue with the `conduct` label
2. **Email**: Contact project maintainers directly
3. **Direct Message**: Reach out to any project maintainer

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality.

### What to Include in a Report

When reporting a violation, please include:

- Your contact information
- Names (usernames) of individuals involved
- Description of the incident
- Links to relevant content (issues, comments, etc.)
- Any additional context or screenshots
- Whether you'd like to remain anonymous

### Investigation Process

1. **Acknowledgment**: Report will be acknowledged within 24-48 hours
2. **Investigation**: Maintainers will review the incident
3. **Decision**: Appropriate action will be determined
4. **Communication**: Reporter will be informed of the outcome
5. **Appeal**: Decisions may be appealed if new information emerges

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these guidelines in determining consequences:

### 1. Correction

**Behavior**: Use of inappropriate language or other unprofessional behavior.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Behavior**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces and external channels. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Behavior**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Behavior**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment, or aggression.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## Consequences for Violations

Depending on the severity and nature of the violation, consequences may include:

- **Informal Warning**: For minor first-time violations
- **Formal Warning**: Documented warning for future reference
- **Temporary Suspension**: Time-limited ban from contributing
- **Permanent Ban**: Complete removal from the community
- **Reporting to Authorities**: In cases of illegal activity

## Appeals Process

If you believe you've been unfairly accused or penalized:

1. Submit an appeal to the project maintainers
2. Provide additional context or evidence
3. Wait for review by a different maintainer
4. Accept the final decision
